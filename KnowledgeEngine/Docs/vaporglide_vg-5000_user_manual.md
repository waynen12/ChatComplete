## Page 1 of 20 — Welcome

### What you’ve got
The **VaporGlide™ VG‑5000** is a multi‑function steam cleaning system designed for:
- Sealed hard floors (tile, sealed laminate, sealed vinyl, sealed stone)
- Grout lines, taps/fixtures, cooktop splatter, and other hard surfaces (with handheld mode)
- Refreshing carpets/rugs (with carpet glider) — *light refresh only*
- Garments and soft furnishings (with fabric tool) — *steam refresh; not a dry cleaner*

Steam cleaning can reduce reliance on chemical cleaners for routine cleaning.

## Page 2 of 20 — Safety Summary (Read First)

> **WARNING — HOT STEAM**
Steam can cause severe burns. Never point steam at people, pets, or plants.

> **WARNING — ELECTRICAL SAFETY**
- Use only a properly grounded 230V outlet.
- **Unplug before filling the tank, changing accessories, or cleaning the unit.**
- Do not immerse the unit or plug in water.

> **CAUTION — SURFACE COMPATIBILITY**
- **Do not use on unsealed wood/parquet** or floors with gaps, raised edges, or damaged finish.
- When in doubt, check the flooring manufacturer’s instructions.

> **CAUTION — DO NOT LEAVE UNATTENDED**
Do not leave the appliance unattended while plugged in.

> **CAUTION — COOL DOWN**
Allow the unit and accessories to cool before removing pads or attachments.

### Safety checklist
Before each use:
- Confirm surface is suitable for steam.
- Inspect power cord for damage.
- Check that pads/attachments are correctly installed.
- Ensure the water tank cap is sealed.

During use:
- Keep hands away from steam outlet.
- Move continuously; **do not hold steam in one spot**.
- Keep cord behind you and away from hot surfaces.

After use:
- Turn off, unplug, let cool, empty tank, and dry pads.

## Page 6 of 20 — Getting Started (Floor Mode)

### First‑use prep
- Wash microfiber pads once before first use.
- Vacuum/sweep the floor first (steam mops aren’t designed for picking up grit).

### Start cleaning
1. Plug in.
2. Press **Power**.
3. Wait for **Ready** light (solid).
4. Select steam level:
   - **Low**: sealed wood/laminate (if permitted), quick refresh
   - **Medium**: tiles/vinyl daily clean
   - **High**: tough grime on durable sealed surfaces

### Technique
- Move in steady strokes.
- For stuck marks: hover **briefly** and wipe, but **don’t dwell** in one spot.
- For edges: angle floor head so pad contacts the edge.

> **NOTE:** If you see droplets, the unit may not be fully heated or the level is too high for the surface.

## Page 9 of 20 — Carpet & Rug Refresh (Carpet Glider)

The carpet glider helps the mop head **slide** on carpet while allowing steam to penetrate fibres for a **light refresh**.

### How to use
1. Attach a clean microfiber pad to the floor head.
2. Place the floor head into the **carpet glider** until it sits flat.
3. Use **Low** or **Medium** steam.
4. Move continuously in slow passes.

### Don’ts
- Do not use on delicate rugs without testing.
- Do not saturate carpet.
- Do not use to treat heavy stains—use a dedicated carpet cleaner for that.

## Page 10 of 20 — Surface Guide

### Generally suitable (sealed surfaces)
- Ceramic/porcelain tile
- Sealed vinyl / LVP (manufacturer permitting)
- Sealed stone (test first)
- Sealed laminate (avoid seams; low steam)

### Use caution / test first
- Sealed hardwood (only if floor manufacturer permits; lowest steam; quick passes)
- Painted surfaces (test in hidden area)
- Grout with crumbling mortar (steam may loosen further)

### Not suitable
- **Unsealed wood or parquet**
- Waxed floors
- Floors with gaps, lifted edges, or water damage
- Leather (use only approved methods)
- Silk/velvet or delicate fabrics (use only if garment label allows steam)

### Quick test
Steam a hidden area for 10 seconds, let dry, check for warping, dulling, or adhesive softening.

## Page 11 of 20 — Cleaning Patterns & Best Practices

### Routine (weekly)
1. Dry debris removal: vacuum/sweep.
2. Steam mop on **Medium** (or **Low** for sensitive surfaces).
3. Replace pad if it becomes heavily soiled.

### Kitchen grease & footprints
- Use **High** on durable sealed tile.

### Bathrooms
- Handheld mode with brush for grout.
- Finish with floor mode for overall clean.

### Tips for better results
- Start with clean pad.
- Use overlapping strokes.
- Swap pads for large areas (dirty pads smear grime).

## Page 14 of 20 — Descaling (Limescale Prevention)

> **NOTE:** Scale depends on local water hardness.

### Best prevention
- Use **distilled/demineralised water**.

### When to descale
- Reduced steam output
- Sputtering/water spitting
- Ready light takes longer than normal
- After ~10–20 hours in hard‑water areas

### Descaling method (recommended)
1. Unplug and cool completely.
2. Empty tank.
3. Add **VaporGlide Descale Solution** (or manufacturer‑approved equivalent) per label.
4. Let sit 30 minutes.
5. Empty and rinse tank thoroughly (2–3 rinses).
6. Fill with clean water and run steam for 1–2 minutes over a sink (handheld mode) to flush.

> **CAUTION:** Do not use strong acids unless approved for your device. Unapproved solutions may damage seals and void warranty.

## Page 15 of 20 — Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No steam, Ready light off | Not powered | Check outlet, plug, Power button |
| Ready light blinking | Heating | Wait 25–35 seconds |
| Low steam / sputtering | Low water or scale | Refill; descale (Page 14) |
| Water drips from nozzle | Not fully heated or tilted | Wait for Ready; keep level; reduce steam |
| Leaves streaks on floor | Dirty pad / too much steam | Replace pad; reduce steam; finish with clean pad |
| Unit turns off | Overheat protection | Unplug, cool 30 min, restart; ensure vents clear |
| Carpet feels damp | Too much steam / slow movement | Use Low; move continuously; allow dry time |
| Glass streaks | Dirty blade / hard water | Wipe blade; finish with microfiber cloth |

If issue persists: stop using and contact service (Page 19).

## Page 16 of 20 — Error Indicators

### Low water light blinking
- Tank empty or not seated.
- Refill and click tank in place.

### Rapid Ready light flash (3×, repeat)
- Overheat detected.
- Turn off, unplug, cool fully, check vents.

### Alternating lights
- Sensor fault (rare).
- Power cycle; if repeated, contact service.
